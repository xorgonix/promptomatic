Hi all,


It's been quite a time since I did a circular email with any update re: Mary, and apologies for that. In part, it's because there has not been a lot to day, and in part I have just not got round to it, for which I do apologise.


Anyway hopefully you will be able to see the image attached of a visit to Mary over Christmas, featuring Jennie and Alexander. This was one of the better days on average in terms of visiting, Mary was bright and alert and full of conversation, even remembering one or two names unprompted. It would be fair to say that this experience is now very much in the minority, as most often she is either very sleepy and/or not really willing or able to engage.


But that said it is clear to us that she does continue to appreciate people visiting, and we know from seeing various cards and messages that people do come and see her from time to time, as well as write, and we thank you very much for doing so, especially as we know it's a lottery as to which Mary you may get on the day.


In herself Mary is physically pretty stable, she had a chesty cold pre-Christmas (who didn't...??) and was treated with antibiotics but was soon over the symptoms. There has been no change to her mobility, as in, she has none independently, and the only observable change is the very slow rate at which one notices that her memory and ability to engage with anyone around her continues to decline.


The staff at Chaseview are very caring and supportive, and it's clear to us that they like Mary, although at times she undoubtedly presents them with some challenge.


Anyway, many thanks again to those of you who write and/or visit, if there's any significant change in how things are I will drop a note around.


In the meantime, we wish you all a very happy and peaceful New Year.


Kind regards to all,


Martin & Sally
